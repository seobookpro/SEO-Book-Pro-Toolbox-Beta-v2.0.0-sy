Key Concepts to Master

Keywords & Search Intent

Keywords are the words and phrases people type into search engines. But it's not enough to just know the keywords; you must understand the Search Intent behind them. Why is the user searching for this? Are they looking to buy something (transactional), learn something (informational), find a specific website (navigational), or research before buying (commercial)? Matching your content to the user's intent is critical for ranking well.

E-A-T: Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness

E-A-T is part of Google's framework for evaluating content quality. It's especially important for topics that could impact a person's health or finances (called "Your Money or Your Life" or YMYL pages).

  • Expertise: The content is created by someone with proven knowledge in the field.
  • Authoritativeness: The website and author are recognized as a go-to source for the topic. This is often demonstrated by backlinks from other respected sites.
  • Trustworthiness: The website is secure (HTTPS), has clear contact information, and presents information accurately and honestly.

How Search Engines Work: Crawling, Indexing, Ranking

Search engines work in three main stages:

  1. Crawling: Search engine bots (spiders) discover publicly available webpages by following links.
  2. Indexing: They process and store the information from these pages in a massive database called an index.
  3. Ranking: When a user performs a search, the search engine scours its index for the most relevant pages and displays them in order of relevance on the search results page.

Your job in SEO is to make all three of these steps as easy as possible for search engines.
